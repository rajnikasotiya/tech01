### 1. Three Problems That Are Inappropriate to Solve with Generative AI

1. **Making Fully Autonomous Medical Decisions Without Human Oversight**
   Generative AI can assist doctors by summarizing patient records, identifying trends, or explaining medical concepts, but it should not independently diagnose illnesses or make final treatment decisions without expert review. AI models can produce incorrect or hallucinated information, may miss important context, and do not truly understand patient conditions. In healthcare, even small mistakes can lead to serious consequences. From the course, one important lesson was that generative AI works best as an assistant for humans rather than as a replacement for expert judgment in high-risk environments.

2. **High-Stakes Legal or Financial Decisions**
   Generative AI should not be trusted to independently create legally binding contracts, provide final legal rulings, or make investment decisions involving significant financial risk. AI systems may generate convincing but inaccurate information, misunderstand laws, or fail to account for regional regulations and recent legal updates. For example, relying completely on AI for tax filings, compliance decisions, or investment strategies could create serious legal and financial problems. Human professionals are still required to verify accuracy, context, and ethical considerations.

3. **Deciding Hiring, Promotions, or Sensitive Human Evaluations Without Transparency**
   Using generative AI as the sole decision-maker for hiring, employee evaluations, admissions, or promotions is inappropriate because AI systems can inherit bias from training data and may produce unfair or non-transparent decisions. While AI can help summarize resumes, identify skills, or assist with screening, humans should remain responsible for final evaluations. The course emphasized that AI outputs should always be reviewed critically, especially when fairness, ethics, and human opportunities are involved.

Overall, generative AI is most effective when used as a productivity and decision-support tool rather than an unquestioned authority. Human oversight, validation, and accountability remain essential for sensitive or high-impact decisions.

### 2. ACHIEVE Framework Examples

The ACHIEVE framework can help structure how generative AI is applied to real-world problems in a practical and responsible way. Below are examples related to AI projects, healthcare analytics, and personal productivity.

* **A – Automate Repetitive Tasks**
  I can use generative AI to automate repetitive documentation tasks in healthcare analytics projects, such as summarizing medical claim documents, extracting important fields, and generating structured reports automatically from uploaded PDFs.

* **C – Collect and Organize Information**
  AI can help gather and organize information from research papers, meeting notes, datasets, and project documentation into searchable summaries or dashboards. For example, I could upload NLP research papers and ask AI to generate categorized notes and implementation ideas.

* **H – Help with Creativity and Ideation**
  Generative AI is useful for brainstorming project ideas, designing AI workflows, generating presentation outlines, and creating content for LinkedIn or technical blogs. It can quickly generate multiple approaches to solving AI or automation problems.

* **I – Improve Communication**
  AI can rewrite technical explanations for non-technical audiences, improve email drafts, summarize meetings, and generate professional presentations. This is especially useful when communicating AI concepts to business stakeholders or healthcare teams.

* **E – Enhance Learning and Skill Development**
  I can use AI to explain difficult machine learning concepts, generate coding exercises, create interview questions, and provide personalized study plans for NLP, FastAPI, or Generative AI topics.

* **V – Visualize Data and Insights**
  Using tools like Code Interpreter, AI can automatically generate charts, dashboards, and PowerPoint slides from datasets. For example, healthcare claim trends or model evaluation metrics can be visualized for presentations and analysis.

* **E – Evaluate and Refine Solutions**
  AI can help compare multiple machine learning models, summarize evaluation metrics, suggest optimizations, and identify weaknesses in workflows or project architectures. This supports continuous improvement in AI systems and operational processes.

The ACHIEVE framework demonstrates that generative AI is most valuable when applied thoughtfully to enhance productivity, creativity, learning, communication, and decision support while keeping humans actively involved in reviewing and guiding outcomes.
