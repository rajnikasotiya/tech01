### Question 1: Understand the scenario (100–150 words)

Deploying an ML model to optimize energy usage in a smart city presents several important challenges. The first challenge is data privacy, as the system collects detailed energy consumption data from households and businesses. Improper handling of this data could expose personal behavior patterns and create security risks. The second challenge is equity, where the ML model may prioritize energy distribution to certain neighborhoods while underserved regions receive fewer benefits, leading to unfair access to resources. The third challenge is cost management because implementing smart sensors, cloud infrastructure, and continuous model maintenance requires significant financial investment. These challenges can impact public trust, government policies, and long-term sustainability. Therefore, the deployment must balance efficiency, fairness, privacy protection, and operational affordability to ensure successful adoption across the smart city ecosystem.

---

### Question 2: Analyze the challenges (100–150 words)

One major implication of deploying the ML model is the risk of privacy violations for users if sensitive energy usage data is leaked or misused. Utility companies and governments may also face legal and reputational consequences if proper security measures are not implemented. Another implication is inequitable energy distribution, where biased models could allocate resources unfairly across regions or income groups, negatively affecting public trust and social equality. Environmental sustainability may also suffer if certain areas experience inefficient energy optimization. The third implication is increased operational and infrastructure costs due to smart devices, cloud computing, and ongoing system maintenance. To address these challenges, organizations should implement strong encryption, anonymization, fairness-aware AI models, and cost-efficient cloud infrastructure with auto-scaling capabilities. Continuous monitoring and audits can further improve transparency, reliability, and long-term sustainability.

---

### Question 3: Propose solutions (100–150 words)

To address data privacy concerns, the smart city can implement differential privacy, encryption, and secure access controls to protect user energy consumption data. These techniques reduce the risk of unauthorized access while maintaining useful insights for ML predictions. To ensure equity, fairness-aware machine learning algorithms and regular bias audits should be used so that energy optimization benefits are distributed equally across all communities and demographics. Transparent reporting can also improve public trust. For cost management, cloud-based infrastructure with auto-scaling and edge computing can reduce unnecessary operational expenses by allocating resources only when needed. These solutions align with important AI/ML concepts such as ethical AI, responsible data governance, and cost-benefit optimization. By combining privacy protection, fairness, and efficient infrastructure management, the ML deployment can achieve sustainable and socially responsible energy optimization in a smart city.
