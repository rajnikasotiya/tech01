### 1. Filtering and Traceability Prompt

**Prompt:**
“Analyze the uploaded healthcare claims dataset and filter all claims with bill amounts greater than $10,000 and approval status marked as ‘Pending’ or ‘Rejected.’ For every filtered result, maintain traceability by preserving the original claim identifiers, member IDs, provider IDs, and claim dates from the source data. Generate a summary table showing the filtered claims, the reason they were selected, and any detected anomalies or missing fields. Do not remove or modify source identifiers. Include a section mapping every generated insight back to the original claim ID for auditing purposes.”

**Sample Output:**
The analysis identified 24 healthcare claims with bill amounts exceeding $10,000 that were either pending or rejected. All original identifiers, including claim IDs, member IDs, provider IDs, and claim dates, were preserved to maintain traceability and support auditing requirements. Several claims were flagged due to missing diagnosis codes, incomplete supporting documents, or inconsistencies between billed services and policy coverage rules.

The filtered results also revealed that multiple rejected claims originated from the same provider group, suggesting possible workflow or documentation issues. By maintaining direct mappings to the original claim identifiers, every generated insight can be traced back to the source records, improving transparency, compliance, and operational review processes.

### 2. Navigation-Based Prompt

**Prompt:**
“You are acting as an AI navigation assistant for a new team member joining an AI healthcare analytics project. Read the uploaded project documentation and generate a structured navigation guide explaining how to move through the project materials. Identify the most important sections, dependencies, workflows, datasets, APIs, and configuration files. Create a step-by-step roadmap showing what a beginner should review first, second, and third in order to understand the system efficiently. Include references to important files and explain how different components connect to each other.”

**Sample Output:**
The project documentation is organized into several major sections, including data ingestion, NLP processing, claims analysis workflows, API integration, and reporting dashboards. A new team member should begin by reviewing the architecture overview and business workflow documents to understand the purpose of the system and how healthcare claims are processed end-to-end.

After reviewing the architecture, the next recommended step is exploring the configuration files and API integration modules, which define how external services and datasets are connected. The documentation also highlights dependencies on provider data, policy rules, and claim validation workflows. Finally, the reporting and visualization section explains how processed claim data is transformed into dashboards and operational insights for analysts and business stakeholders.

This navigation guide provides a structured learning path so new contributors can understand the project more efficiently without becoming overwhelmed by the volume of technical documentation.
